Demand
------

**Demand**

Tariffs: Seasonal TOU Demand,

Code: DEMAND\_ERGON\_SEASONAL_OP

DEMAND\_ERGON\_SEASONAL_PK

Peak demand period is 10.00am to 8.00pm weekdays in Summer

Offpeak demand period is all times Non-Summer

Tariff: Seasonal TOU Demand Residential

Code: DEMAND\_ERGON\_SEASONAL\_RESI\_PK/OP

Peak demand period is 3.00pm to 9.30pm weekdays in Summer

Offpeak demand is all times Non-Summer

**Actual Demand**

The demand used in the calculation of the charge is the maximum demand recorded in any half hour period each month.

Code: ERGON\_DEMAND\_ACTUAL

Tariffs: CAC 66 kV, CAC 33 kV, CAC 22/11 kV Bus, 22/11 kV Line

  
  

DEFINITIONS:

The off-peak demand calculation uses the higher of the maximum kVA demand in any single half hour at any time during each non-summer month and the minimum chargeable demand.

The connection unit calculation applies the connection unit charge (per day) multiplied by the customer's number of connection units as advised individually to each customer.

The off-peak volume calculation uses total metered kWh consumption at all times during non-summer months. For TUOS charges the metered consumption is multiplied by the Distribution Loss Factor and then applied to the TUOS rate.

The peak volume calculation uses total metered kWh consumption at all times during summer months. For TUOS charges the metered consumption is multiplied by the Distribution Loss Factor and then applied to the TUOS rate.

RESIDENTIAL:

The peak demand calculation uses the customer's top four peak demand days (based on daily individual maximum half hour kW demand) in the peak window (3:00pm to 9:30pm). This is a separate calculation in each summer month. The demand charge will be applied to the average kW demand calculated for the total 52 half hour periods each month (i.e. 13 half hour intervals in each peak window multiplied by the four peak demand days).

  
BUSINESS:  

The peak demand calculation uses the customer's top four peak demand days (based on individual half hour demand) in the peak window (10:00am to 8:00pm). This is a separate calculation in each summer month. The demand charge will be applied to the average kW demand calculated for the total 80 half hour periods each month (i.e. 20 half hour intervals in each peak window multiplied by the four peak demand days).

Switching Tariffs
-----------------

Customers essentially assign themselves to a tariff class by selecting the service that they require. Therefore, written notification of the tariff class is not provided by Ergon Energy to the retailer. Changing of tariffs can be done by contacting Ergon directly by phone. No info is available around forms to be completed or expected period of time for tariff change.

If a retailer raises an objection to a tariff class assignment or reassignment for Alternative Control Services, the matter is reviewed and if required, escalated to the  
Manager Regulatory Determination and Pricing for reassessment.
